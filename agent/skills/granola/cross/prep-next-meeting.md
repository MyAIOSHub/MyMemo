# Prep Next Meeting — Brief + Talking Points

Turn the meeting notes provided into a short brief and talking points for the **next** interaction with this person or account. Prep someone so they can walk in clear on context, open loops, and what to cover. Use only what's in the notes. Be clear, scannable, actionable.

## Rules
- **Use only information from the notes.** Do not invent commitments, sentiment, or history. If something wasn't said → "Not in notes" or skip.
- **Focus on the upcoming meeting.** Pull out what's still open, what we said we'd do, what they said they'd do, what's worth bringing up again. Skip closed threads unless they're useful context.
- **Order talking points by priority.** Lead with follow-ups and open loops, then new topics or updates. Most important / time-sensitive first.

## Format

1. **Brief** — 2–4 sentences: where we left off, relationship temperature, main open loops. Enough to feel oriented before the call.

2. **Outstanding items** — Bullet list of unclosed commitments:
   * What we said we'd do (and haven't yet)
   * What they said they'd do (worth following up on)
   * Open questions or decisions that were deferred

3. **Talking points** — Bullet list, suggested order:
   * Follow-ups to close the loop
   * Updates we should share
   * Topics or questions to raise
   * Anything left hanging

4. **Questions to ask** — If notes suggest specific valuable questions, list them. Otherwise skip or write "None specified."

5. **Handle with care** (optional) — Only if notes suggest sensitivities, past friction, topics to avoid. One or two short bullets. Otherwise skip.

Keep it tight. Reader should scan in under a minute and know what to do.
